My cousin Prit had come to India from Germany, and he wanted to make a short trip to Tirupati to visit the Venkateshwara temple there. Since he didn't have any company, and I was jobless anyway, I decided to join him.

It was like divine intervention that a trip to Tirupati materialised out of nowhere - almost as a precursor to my mega journey. We booked ourselves on a 24 hour package tour that would take us from Bangalore on the evening of the 18th and bring us back to Bangalore on the evening of the 19th.

[caption id="attachment_34" align="aligncenter" width="393" caption="Prit & I on the last seat of the bus"][/caption]

[caption id="attachment_38" align="aligncenter" width="405" caption="The beautifully made road to the temple on the hill"][/caption]

The tour bus and the overall service was less than satisfactory, but the darshan itself was something else. I am far from being the religious sorts, but I simply couldn't take my eyes off the black stone idol for the brief 10 seconds at the end of the long 4 hour wait in queue. Somehow, the long wait seemed to be worth it.
